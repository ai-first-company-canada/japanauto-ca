## What is Kei Car?

Kei cars are a uniquely Japanese vehicle category covering small passenger cars (e.g., Honda N-Box), kei trucks (Honda Acty), and kei vans. They benefit from reduced Japanese road tax, parking exemptions, and yellow licence plates. Japanese-brand whitelist kei vehicles relevant to Canadian buyers are primarily Honda kei trucks (Acty) and the Honda Beat / S660 sports kei.

## Why it matters in Canada

Kei vehicles are importable to Canada under the 15-year RIV rule, and several Canadian provinces — notably British Columbia and Quebec — accept kei trucks for off-highway and limited on-highway use. They are not legal for full highway use in some provinces (Ontario has historically been restrictive on right-hand-drive imports). Kei trucks are popular on Canadian farms, ski resorts, and golf courses for their low cost and small footprint.
