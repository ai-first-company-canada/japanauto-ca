## What is JDM?

JDM is the term for cars and components sold new only in Japan. JDM vehicles often feature right-hand drive, distinct trim packages, unique engine variants (e.g., Honda Type R, Nissan Skyline GT-R, Toyota Chaser), and Japan-specific safety and emissions equipment. In Canada, JDM vehicles are legally importable once they reach the 15-year age threshold under Transport Canada's Registrar of Imported Vehicles (RIV) rules.

## Why it matters in Canada

Canada's 15-year import rule (vs. the 25-year rule in the United States) makes Canada one of the most permissive JDM markets in North America, and Vancouver and Toronto are the two largest JDM import hubs. Importing requires a Form 1, federal inspection exemption, provincial safety certification (e.g., OMVIC-aligned safety in Ontario, AMVIC-aligned in Alberta), and registration via the RIV programme.
