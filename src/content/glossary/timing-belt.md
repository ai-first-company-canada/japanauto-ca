## What is Timing Belt?

Timing belts are interference-engine-critical components: failure on most modern Japanese interference engines (e.g., Honda J-series V6, older Toyota 2GR-FE) causes piston-to-valve contact and major engine damage. Many Japanese manufacturers transitioned from timing belts to maintenance-free timing chains during the 2000s — modern Toyota A25A, Honda K20C, and most Mazda SkyActiv engines now use chains, while specific Honda V6 (J35) Odyssey, Pilot, and Acura MDX engines still use belts.

## Why it matters in Canada

Timing-belt service intervals on Japanese vehicles in Canada are typically 100,000–168,000 km depending on model — the Honda J-series interval is 168,000 km or 8 years. Skipped service is a leading cause of catastrophic engine failure on used Honda Odyssey, Pilot, and Acura MDX listings, so service history is the most material due-diligence item for these models on japanauto.ca.
