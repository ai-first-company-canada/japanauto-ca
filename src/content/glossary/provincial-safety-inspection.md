## What is Provincial Safety Inspection?

Each province sets its own inspection regime: Ontario uses the Safety Standards Certificate (SSC) at vehicle transfer; British Columbia requires an inspection only for designated vehicles via Designated Inspection Facilities; Alberta requires an Out-of-Province Inspection (OOPI) for any vehicle entering the province; Quebec uses the SAAQ mechanical inspection for non-Quebec-origin vehicles. JDM imports require a federal RIV inspection plus the relevant provincial safety.

## Why it matters in Canada

Provincial safety standards are the gating step between a vehicle entering Canada (or moving between provinces) and being road-legal. Ontario's SSC is valid for 36 days from issue; Alberta's OOPI must be performed by a licensed AMVIC inspection facility. Buyers on japanauto.ca cross-shopping vehicles from another province should budget 100–250 CAD plus repair costs for the inspection step.
